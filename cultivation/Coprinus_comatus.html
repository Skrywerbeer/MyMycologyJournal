Coprinus comatus

Probably one of the easest mushrooms to identify. Its rapdily liquifying body and true to its name shaggy appearance, although I have noticed that very small fruit bodies have smooth caps still maintaining the telltale elongated shape.

A sort of spore print that cultures can be started from, can be made by letting a drop of the black goo drip onto a sheet of aluminium foil. Try to keep the conditions and surfaces as clean as possible also keep the sample covered while allowing for air flow as it is drying to minimize the number of contaminants. Folded over and the edges lightly crimped, the foil is easily stored.

A culture will grow happily on any grain and will even fruit once all of the substrate has been colonized.
