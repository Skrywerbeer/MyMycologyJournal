Plearotus ostreatus

A friend of a friend that also has an interest in mushrooms was kind enough to gift me a piece of oyster mushroom from his harvest. I started my culture by placing a largish piece above an open, sterile jar with barley as substrate. This setup was left inside my oven for a day. This left the top surface of the substrate covered with a thick layer of light grey spores. In retrospect waiting a whole day might be excessive given how many spores dropped P. ostreatus's reputation for heavy spore load.

Subsequent subculturing has left me with a strong strain that I have even witnesed defeating Aspergillus mold.
