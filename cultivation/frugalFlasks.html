I love peanut butter, I can't get enough of it(unsalted and unsweetened only). Thanks to this and the fact that a certain brandüêà sells theirs in glass jars; I have a near constant supply of flasks for culturing fungi in.

With a small 6 mm hole drilled in the lid plugged with a ball of polyester filter floss. I have yet to experiment with the hole size but so far 6 mm has proven more than sufficient and is a convienent size for a decent ball of floss. The even has the advantage of being injectable if you happen to have liquid culture.

If you lose the lid or the hole rusts away and becomes too big. A single layer of aluminium foil that is tied down with twine after inoculation and poked through with a needle for gas exchange is a workable substitute, not as convenient because you have to undo the tiwne each time to open the jar. The floss will not work for the foil. Use paper towel folded once or twice with the edges taped down instead. And if you kept your pin pricks confined to a small area you only need stamp sized square.

I have yet to try it myself, but the cloth used for waterproofing roofs is made of polyester and might be a reusable, pressure cooker proof alternative to paper.

There are fancier ways to achieve the same thing; actual labratory glassware, silicone ports, etc. but are they needed? For amateur cultivationn not really.

Of course it doesn't have to be a peanut butter jar; any glass recepticale of reasonable geometry that fits in your pressure cooker will work. I just like the ones filled with peanut butter the most.
