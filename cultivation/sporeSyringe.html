Aluminium foil is a great substrate for making spore prints. The metallic surface allows most colours of spores to clearly show, no need for white and black paper. It is waterproof and can be steam cleaned. Also it is Airtight and unreactive under the conditions considered. The waterproof nature also makes it easy to efficiently use the spores for creating cultures.

Fill a syringe with sterile water, if you just finished sterilizing jars filled with growth medium you have ample sterile water in the pot, put a drop of water somewhere on the edge of the print and using the needle gently mix up the spores into the droplet. When the spores have been freely dispersed suck up the drop keeping the needle bevel faced down to maximize the amount of water pulled up. Gently turn the syringe upside down and back again a few times to distribute the spores into the full volume of the water, having an air bubble in the barrel helps to speed up the mixing.

Evenly inoculating growth medium is now as easy as squirting some liquid over it. I find 1 cc syringes a convenient size and the small gauge needle squirts a fine stream with a controlable low flow rate.
